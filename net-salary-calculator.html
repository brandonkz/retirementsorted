How Net Salary Is Calculated in South Africa (2026/2027)

Your net salary (also called take-home pay) is what you actually receive in your bank account each month after all deductions. In South Africa, the main deductions from your gross salary are:

  • PAYE (Pay As You Earn) — income tax calculated using SARS tax brackets
  • UIF (Unemployment Insurance Fund) — 1% of gross salary, capped at R177.12/month
  • Pension/Retirement Fund — if you contribute to a pension or retirement annuity

You may also receive medical aid tax credits which reduce your PAYE tax and increase your take-home pay.

Deductions & Credits

  • UIF: 1% of gross salary (capped at R177.12/month)
  • Medical Aid Tax Credit: R376/month for main member, R376 for first dependant, R254 each additional dependant
  • Pension/RA: Contributions reduce taxable income (max 27.5% of income, capped at R430,000/year)
  • Annual Tax Rebate: R17,820 (already factored into PAYE calculations)

Frequently Asked Questions

How is net salary calculated in South Africa?

Net salary = Gross salary - PAYE tax - UIF + Medical aid tax credits. Your PAYE is calculated using SARS progressive tax brackets, meaning higher portions of your income are taxed at higher rates. The 2026/2027 primary rebate of R17,820 means you pay no tax on the first ~R99,000 of annual income.

What is the take-home pay on a R30,000 salary in South Africa?

On a gross monthly salary of R30,000 (R360,000/year), you'll pay approximately R4,681 in PAYE and R177 in UIF, giving you a net take-home of about R25,142 per month. With medical aid for one person, it's about R25,518. Use the calculator above for your exact figures.

What is the take-home pay on a R50,000 salary?

On a gross monthly salary of R50,000 (R600,000/year), you'll pay approximately R11,076 in PAYE and R177 in UIF, giving you a net take-home of about R38,747 per month. Contributing R5,000/month to a retirement annuity would reduce your tax and increase your net pay.

How much can I save on tax with a retirement annuity?

SARS allows you to deduct up to 27.5% of your taxable income (max R430,000/year) for retirement fund contributions. This reduces your taxable income before PAYE is calculated. For someone earning R50,000/month, a R5,000/month RA contribution could save you roughly R1,800 in tax per month depending on your bracket.

What is the tax threshold in South Africa for 2026/2027?

For the 2026/2027 tax year, you don't pay income tax if you earn below R99,000 per year (about R8,250/month) if you're under 65. For ages 65-74, the threshold is R153,250, and for 75+, it's R171,300. These thresholds are based on the primary, secondary, and tertiary rebates.

Is this calculator accurate for 2026/2027?

Yes. This calculator uses the official SARS 2026/2027 tax brackets, rebates, UIF rates, and medical aid tax credits. It's updated annually when SARS publishes new rates (usually during the February Budget Speech). For complex situations (multiple income sources, capital gains, foreign income), consult a tax professional.
