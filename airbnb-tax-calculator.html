What This Means for Airbnb Owners

Who's affected:

  • Properties used primarily for short-term letting (Airbnb, Booking.com)
  • Over 25,000 listings in Cape Town
  • Areas like Sea Point, City Bowl, Woodstock, Atlantic Seaboard

Key numbers:

  • Current residential rate: 0.86 cents per rand (first R300k rebate)
  • Proposed commercial rate: ~2.0 cents per rand (no rebate)
  • Increase: Approximately 135% higher annual rates

Example impact (R2M property):

  • Currently pays R14,572/year (R1,214/month)
  • Under new rules would pay R40,280/year (R3,357/month)
  • Increase of R25,708/year (R2,142/month)

City's reasoning:

  • "Airbnbs operate like decentralized hotels" - should pay same rates
  • Encourage properties back into long-term rental market
  • Address Cape Town's housing shortage
  • Level playing field with hotels/guesthouses

Your options:

  • Continue as short-term rental: Accept higher rates if income justifies it
  • Switch to long-term rental: Keep residential rates but lose Airbnb income
  • Hybrid approach: Rent long-term with occasional short-term (check legal classification)
  • Sell the property: Exit the market entirely

Timeline:

  • Policy still under consultation (as of Feb 2026)
  • Not yet law - implementation date TBC
  • Likely to face legal challenges from property owners
  • Stay updated via City of Cape Town announcements
